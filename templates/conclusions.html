Conclusions

25% of customers remain in contract for less than 9 months -> take a closer look at the first months.

Mean of monthly charges is lower than median -> compare customer profiles of groups with low and high monthly fees.

The longer the contract has lasted, the fewer contract terminations there are - > compare the profiles of customers with short-term and long-term contracts.

Except for a group with a very high monthly fee, there are more contract terminations as the monthly fee increases.

The highest total revenue is brought by customers with higher monthly fees.

The client groups with the lowest monthly fee are under 65 years of age, without a partner, has dependents.

The client groups that generate the highest total revenue are those over the age of 65, have a partner, without dependents

The contract is terminated more by long-term clients with a partner, clients over 65 years of age with a higher average monthly fee. In all sociodemographic groups, the higher average total revenue is bought by customers who have terminated the contract.

Customers with a higher monthly fee have phone, fiber optic and TV service.

Customers with higher total revenue who have not terminated the contract have phone service, fiber optic or dsl and tv service.

In the group of customers with high total revenue who have not terminated the contract, there are also those who do not have a phone or TV service.

The customers with the highest total revenue are under 65 who has all three services.


Next steps for analysis

Profiles and services of customers who terminate service within 9 months.

Profiles of customers low and high monthly fees.

The profitability or non profitability of customers over 65 years of age.

Analyse the relevance of tv service.
